Business Models
We Support

The Hyper-Local Model

Deliver goods within a small radius for ultra-fast and efficient local service. Perfect for groceries, restaurants, and pharmacies.

E.g. Dunzo (India), Gopuff (USA)

Subscription Model

Recurring deliveries for essential or curated products, ensuring convenience and customer loyalty. Ideal for food, groceries, and medicines.

E.g. Blue Apron, HelloFresh, Dollar Shave Club

Multi-Chain Model

Ideal for large businesses with multiple store locations, ensuring seamless operations and delivery management.

E.g. Blinkit (India), FreshDirect (USA)

The Marketplace Model

Connects multiple vendors with customers, providing a wide variety of choices in one convenient platform.

E.g. Uber Eats, DoorDash, Amazon Fresh

Multi-Service Aggregator Model

Combines multiple delivery services—food, groceries, courier, and more—into a single powerful platform.

E.g. Gojek (Indonesia), Rappi (Latin America)

Cloud Kitchen / Dark Kitchen

Operates delivery-only kitchens without dine-in, optimizing food service for online orders.

E.g. Rebel Foods (India), CloudKitchens (USA), Faasos

The Inventory Model

Businesses manage their own stock and fulfillment, ensuring product availability and direct delivery.

E.g. Blinkit (India), FreshDirect (USA)

Business-to-Business (B2B) Model

Enables bulk deliveries between businesses, streamlining supply chains for wholesalers and retailers.

E.g. Udaan (India), Faire (USA), Metro (Europe)

Direct-to-Customer (D2C) Model

Brands sell and deliver directly to consumers, ensuring control over customer experience and branding.

E.g. Nike, Apple, Casper

On-Demand Model

Offers instant delivery for urgent needs, perfect for courier, medicine, and last-mile services.

E.g. Postmates (USA), Zepto (India)

Logistics Delivery Model

Built for speed and flexibility, this model enables businesses to deliver goods directly from nearby hubs or stores to customers-either instantly or on a scheduled basis. It ensures faster fulfillment, real-time tracking, and a superior customer experience for urgent and everyday deliveries.

E.g. Amazon Logistics, Postmates, Gopuff

The Shopping Model (Customer First)

Prioritizes personalized shopping experiences, offering curated selections and tailored recommendations for customers.

E.g. Instacart (USA), BigBasket (India)

What are the essential features of a successful food delivery app?

A successful food delivery app should include a user-friendly interface, real-time order tracking, secure payment gateways, and efficient delivery management system. Other key features include the ability to schedule deliveries, multiple payment options, and customer reviews. A robust food delivery app is crucial for ensuring timely deliveries, enhancing customer satisfaction, and optimizing delivery routes for efficiency.

What is Q-Commerce (Quick Commerce)?

Q-Commerce, or Quick Commerce, is the next evolution of e-commerce that focuses on delivering products to customers in the shortest time possible—often within 30 minutes to an hour. It blends the convenience of traditional e-commerce with the speed of on-demand delivery, catering to the growing consumer demand for instant gratification.
